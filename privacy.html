PagePilot AI — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 16, 2026. Applies to PagePilot AI version 1.0.0.

PagePilot AI ("the extension") is a Chrome extension that summarizes the webpage you are viewing and lets you chat with and save those summaries. It is designed so that the developer learns nothing about you.

What the extension stores on your device

Using chrome.storage.local, the extension stores: your settings (auto mode, summary language); your license record; a device identifier used only for free daily-limit counting; a daily usage counter (date, count, last summarized URL); and your saved-summary library (URLs, titles, summaries, key points). This data never leaves your device. You can delete the library at any time from Settings, and removing the extension deletes all of it.

What leaves your device, and when

Only the following network requests are ever made, each initiated by your actions:

  1. Summaries and chat → PagePilot backend → OpenAI. When a summary or chat answer is generated (by you, or by auto mode when you open an article), the extracted text of the page you are viewing is sent from your browser to the PagePilot backend at server-eight-ivory-88.vercel.app. The backend forwards that text to api.openai.com using PagePilot's key and returns the result to you; it does not store article text. OpenAI's processing of that request is governed by their own terms and privacy policy.
  2. Pro activation → PagePilot backend. When you subscribe to Pro on Gumroad, the Gumroad sale_id is sent from the post-purchase success page to the PagePilot backend at server-eight-ivory-88.vercel.app. This backend is a small Vercel serverless function backed by Upstash Redis; it only stores the sale/subscription id and activation status needed to verify the subscription. No page content or personal information are sent.
  3. Legacy license-key activation → Gumroad. The optional manual key entry in Settings sends your license key and the product id pagepilot-ai to api.gumroad.com for verification. This is only used if automatic activation fails.

What the extension does NOT do

  • No accounts, sign-ups, or identifiers beyond the anonymous device id for free-limit counting.
  • No telemetry, analytics, crash reporting, or tracking of any kind.
  • No browsing-history access: the extension only ever reads the single page you currently have open, at the moment a summary is requested.
  • No sale, rental, or transfer of any user data to any third party for any purpose unrelated to the extension's core function.

Permissions

The extension's permissions (sidePanel, storage, activeTab, tabs, scripting, contextMenus, and host access to all URLs, api.gumroad.com, and server-eight-ivory-88.vercel.app) are each required for the single purpose described above and are used for nothing else.

Children

The extension does not knowingly collect information from anyone, including children under 13, because it collects no information at all.

Changes

Any change to this policy will ship with a new extension version and be visible in the repository's CHANGELOG.
